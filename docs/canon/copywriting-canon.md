# ADIGO — Canonical Copywriting Framework

Applies to: Web (public), Web App (user-facing), Mobile Web
Audience: U.S. gig workers, independent contractors, 1099 professionals
Tone: Professional, credible, supportive, advocacy-driven
Avoid: hype, slang, exaggerated claims, startup buzzwords

1) Global Rules
- Voice: serious but human; supportive, not salesy; plain English; speak with workers
- Language: short sentences; active voice; no exclamation points; avoid hype terms; no unverifiable promises
- Terminology: “Gig workers and independent contractors”; “Membership”; “Benefits and support”; “Coverage options”

2) Site-Wide Microcopy
- Primary CTAs: Join ADIGO, Become a Member, Get Started, Learn More
- Secondary: View details, See how it works, Contact support, Review options

3) Home — Canonical Copy
- Headline: Support, benefits, and advocacy for gig workers and independent contractors.
- Subheadline: ADIGO helps independent workers access benefits, resources, and representation—without giving up flexibility.
- Primary CTA: Become a Member; Secondary: Learn how ADIGO works
- Values: Support, Benefits, Advocacy blocks; Trust statement (no reclassification)

4) About Us — Canonical Copy
- About ADIGO; Narrative; Mission statement

5) Services — Canonical Copy
- Health & Coverage Options; Financial & Retirement; Legal; Education & Workshops; Community & Advocacy

6) Service Details — Structure
- What This Is; Who It’s For; How It Helps; CTA: Become a Member to Learn More

7) Contact Us
- “Have questions… We’re here to help.” Confirmation: “Thank you…”

8) Terms & Conditions — Guidance
- Neutral, clear, no marketing language

9) Footer
- Tagline (optional): Supporting independent workers nationwide.
- Legal: © ADIGO. All rights reserved.

10) Mobile Adjustments
- Shorten headings; avoid multi‑sentence paragraphs; prefer bullets; CTA ≤3 words (e.g., Join ADIGO)

11) Copy Definition of Done
- Consistency; no contradictions; no guarantees; readable on mobile; clear CTAs
